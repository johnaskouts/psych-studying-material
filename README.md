# **Core books**

#### *D. W. Winnicott — “Ego Distortion in Terms of True and False Self”*

[Probably the closest classic text to “I build a façade to protect the vulnerable self.” Winnicott’s false self is explicitly about a defensive presentation that can hide inner reality.](https://centerforobjectrelations.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/Winnicott-D.W.-1965-The-maturational-processes-and-the-facilitating-environment.-London-Karmac-pp-140-152.pdf)



#### *Erving Goffman — The Presentation of Self in Everyday Life*

[Classic social-psychology/sociology book on how people “perform” versions of themselves in social situations.](https://monoskop.org/images/1/19/Goffman_Erving_The_Presentation_of_Self_in_Everyday_Life.pdf)



#### *Erving Goffman — Stigma: Notes on the Management of Spoiled Identity*

[Useful for understanding hiding, passing, shame, and managing how others perceive you when you feel “different.”](https://ia601503.us.archive.org/22/items/in.ernet.dli.2015.264015/2015.264015.Stigma.pdf)



#### *Carl Jung — writings on the “Persona”*

[Jung’s “persona” is the social mask; useful if you want a symbolic/depth-psychology angle, though it is less empirical than modern research.](https://carljungdepthpsychologysite.blog/2020/02/06/carl-jung-persona/)



#### *R. D. Laing — The Divided Self*

[More existential/clinical; useful for alienation, split identity, and the gap between private self and presented self.](https://raggeduniversity.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/10/Ronald.D.Laing-The.Divided.Self_.1960.pdf)



#### *Alice Miller — The Drama of the Gifted Child*

[Good for “I learned to become what others needed so I could be accepted/loved.” A New Yorker discussion summarizes it as involving a child disguising feelings behind compliance.](https://www.scribd.com/document/780367912/The-Drama-of-the-Gifted-Child-Alice-Miller)



#### *Kelly-Ann Allen — The Psychology of Belonging*

[Short accessible book on belonging, loneliness, groups, identity, and wellbeing.](https://32919420.s21i.faiusr.com/61/ABUIABA9GAAgpLuMvQYol634mgQ.pdf)



#### *Jetten, Haslam \& Haslam — The Social Cure: Identity, Health and Well-Being*

[Academic edited volume on how group memberships and social identities affect mental and physical health.](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Jolanda-Jetten/publication/247446655_The_Social_Cure/links/58d0b571a6fdcc344b0c1364/The-Social-Cure.pdf)



#### *Geoffrey L. Cohen — Belonging: The Science of Creating Connection and Bridging Divides*

[Accessible, research-based book on belonging threats and social connection.](https://wiki.rschooltoday.com/Download_PDFS/uploaded-files/596/782/aN1BYA/belonging-the_science_of_creating_connection_and_bridging_divides_1.pdf)



#### *Richard Schwartz — No Bad Parts*

[Not specifically about masks, but useful for thinking of protective “parts” of the self as trying to keep you safe rather than as enemies.](https://archive.org/details/no-bad-parts-healing-trauma-and-restoring-wholeness-richard-schwartz/No%25252520Bad%25252520Parts%2525253B%25252520Healing%25252520Trauma%25252520and%25252520Restoring%25252520Wholeness%25252520%25252520%252525282021%25252529-Richard%25252520Schwartz/page/n3/mode/2up?ui=embed&wrapper=false)



#### *Roy F. Baumeister \& Steven Scher — “Self-Defeating Behavior Patterns Among Normal Individuals”*

[This is an article, not a book, but it is one of the most important academic pieces. It separates self-defeating behavior into different types: direct self-destruction, harmful tradeoffs, and counterproductive strategies](http://www.ask-force.org/web/Discourse/Baumeister-Self-Defeating-Behaviour-1988.pdf)



#### *Pauline Rose Clance — The Impostor Phenomenon: Overcoming the Fear That Haunts Your Success*

[The original major book by one of the psychologists who coined the concept. Best historical starting point.](https://www.scribd.com/document/797122466/the-impostor-phenomenon-overcoming-the-fear-that-haunts-your-success-0931948770)

#### *Gordon L. Flett \& Paul L. Hewitt — Perfectionism in Childhood and Adolescence*

[Probably the best academic book for perfectionism in children/teens, achievement pressure, mental-health costs, and school/family environments.](https://www.scribd.com/document/945088754/Perfectionism-in-Childhood-and-Adolescence-a-Developmental-Flett-Gordon-L-Hewitt-Paul-L-2021-Dae3202da0c716a365da2c27eae4b72a-Anna)



#### *Jacques Lacan - The Four Fundamental Concepts of Psychoanalysis — Seminar XI*

# **Strong studies / academic articles**

## **Masking, façades, and false self**

#### *Winnicott, “True and False Self”*

[foundational psychoanalytic theory for protective façades.](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Ego+Distortion+in+Terms+of+True+and+False+Self)



#### *Miller et al., “Experiences of Masking in Autistic and Nonautistic Adults”*

[shows masking can involve mimicry, exhaustion, stigma avoidance, and disconnection from identity; relevant even beyond autism, though the autism context is central.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8992921/)



#### *Hull et al., “Putting on My Best Normal”*

[qualitative study on social camouflaging and trying to appear “normal.”](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5509825/)



#### *Lei et al., camouflaging and self/mental health*

[looks at how camouflaging may affect self-concept and mental health beyond simply hiding traits.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11528950/)



#### *Mun \& Kim, false self-presentation on social networking sites*

[connects “lying/false self-presentation” with psychological outcomes online.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12109065/)



### **Self-concealment, stigma, and hiding identity**

#### *Quinn \& Chaudoir, “Living With a Concealable Stigmatized Identity”*

[major framework for how hiding stigmatized parts of self affects wellbeing.](https://rcgd.isr.umich.edu/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/quinn_chaudoir_2009.pdf)



#### *Larson \& Chastain / self-concealment research*

[self-concealment means hiding personal information one sees as distressing or negative.](https://blogs.scu.edu/selfconcealment/files/2013/10/Larson-Chastain-1990-Self-concealment.pdf)



#### *Crabtree \& Pillow, enactment vs concealment and felt authenticity*

[useful for stigma, hiding, authenticity, and motive fulfillment.](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/369970737_Crowded_Conversations_The_Impact_of_Heterogeneous_Audienceson_Belongingness_Distinctiveness_and_Authenticity)



#### *Prizeman et al., stigma, disclosure, and mental-health secrecy in young people with depression*

[good for the shame/secrecy side.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10769096/)



### **Authenticity and self-alienation**

#### *Wood et al., “The Authentic Personality”*

[develops the Authenticity Scale around self-alienation, authentic living, and accepting external influence.](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/42739517_The_Authentic_Personality_A_Theoretical_and_Empirical_Conceptualization_and_the_Development_of_the_Authenticity_Scale)



#### *Goldman \& Kernis, authenticity and healthy psychological functioning*

[links authenticity with self-esteem, life satisfaction, and psychological functioning.](https://d1wqtxts1xzle7.cloudfront.net/49455346/Role_of_Authenticity_in_Healthy_Psycholo20161008-11684-1vhsqtt-libre.pdf?1475946553=&response-content-disposition=inline%2525253B+filename%2525253DRole_of_Authenticity_in_Healthy_Psycholo.pdf&Expires=1778502822&Signature=VMjuzob9ttvvbX4TvcfPlVzbnUoFd4eW~nSJ2HVVwGahDAXB95KSnBbLZPSIXQpKewlXW9i5H0dFa4mx7fSBQ2L8RHFkKchqZxZ2Hp8fPbaG7EAKAcuhTIpt-YJKG927Edm4cJQFRqbUPZvRKT9ZzfVe00ouzlJlMFsHe5182iHzgKCM1u1apt9vwqX1yRhto52UKDmJCpjZeTLO~BbLUgboiu9bjPRP5MUIO0xa5eLvKfyVgBfxPEbqmMKWz-9ZLXXMAOc587SZAld~K4W3~IzJzsMJwyE23DcAfUjUXpkTyQKuVDTUQW3sEOdG~tMNqOqLWcl6Nf4q7M6oWcrNEA__&Key-Pair-Id=APKAJLOHF5GGSLRBV4ZA)



#### *Thomaes et al., adolescent authenticity and wellbeing*

[four studies on how authenticity relates to adolescent wellbeing.](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40894-023-00218-8)



#### *Lutz et al., authenticity, meaning in life, and life satisfaction*

[self-alienation and accepting external influence are negatively related to wellbeing.](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/362042968_Authenticity_Meaning_in_Life_and_Life_Satisfaction_A_Multicomponent_Investigation_of_Relationships_at_the_Trait_and_State_Levels)



### **Belonging, friend groups, and loneliness**

#### *Allen et al., “Belonging: A Review of conceptual issues, an integrative framework, and directions for future research”*

[broad review of belonging theory and research.](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/00049530.2021.1883409#d1e412)



#### *Holt-Lunstad, social connection and health*

[strong overview connecting social isolation/loneliness with mental and physical health.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11403199/)



#### *APA, “The risks of social isolation”*

[accessible article on evidence linking loneliness/social isolation with depression, poor sleep, executive function, and health.](https://www.apa.org/education-career/ce/social-isolation.pdf)



### **Identity crisis / identity confusion**

#### *Marcia’s identity statuses*

[identity diffusion, foreclosure, moratorium, and achievement; helpful for identity crisis language.](https://www.scribd.com/presentation/883753265/Marcia-s-Identity-Statuses)



#### *Ragelienė, identity development and adolescent relationships*

[links identity development with relationship quality and social context.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4879949/)



#### *Bogaerts et al., identity synthesis/confusion and depressive symptoms*

[identity confusion is positively associated with depressive symptoms across adolescence/emerging adulthood.](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1016/j.adolescence.2021.01.006)



### **Isolation, social withdrawal, avoidance, and anhedonia**

#### *Sette et al., motivations for social withdrawal*

[separates shyness, unsociability, and social avoidance in emerging adults.](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-328X/13/12/977)



#### *Barkus \& Badcock, social anhedonia*

[explains reduced pleasure/interest in social contact as distinct from social anxiety.](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2019.00216/full)



#### *Luo et al., social isolation, loneliness, and depressive symptoms*

[longitudinal evidence on loneliness/isolation and depressive symptoms.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36315577/)



### **Protection, shame, attachment, and defenses**

#### *Defense mechanisms — StatPearls / NCBI*

[good clinical overview of unconscious protective strategies.](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK559106/)



#### *Messina et al., attachment orientations and emotion regulation*

[avoidant attachment is associated with defensive over-regulation strategies.](https://www.research.unipd.it/retrieve/66337153-0632-4521-8de6-fa18a8bbbeba/Messina%25252520et%25252520al%25252520%2525282023%252529%25252520Attachment%25252520orientations%25252520and%25252520emotion.pdf)



### **Self-destruction / self-sabotage / self-defeating behavior**=

#### *Ferradás et al., self-esteem, achievement goals, self-handicapping, and defensive pessimism*

[Relevant to perfectionism, low self-esteem, and “I expect failure so I won’t be disappointed.”](https://www.mdpi.com/1660-4601/16/12/2218)



#### *Tsirigotis, “Indirect Self-Destructiveness and Emotional Intelligence”*

[Useful for indirect self-destructive behavior: risk-taking, neglecting health, poor maintenance, interpersonal damage, repeated bad decisions.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4839034/)



#### *Cipriano et al., “Nonsuicidal Self-Injury: A Systematic Review”*

[Only relevant if self-destruction includes self-injury. The review discusses emotion regulation as a common function of NSSI.](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2017.01946/full)



### **Impostor syndrome / impostor phenomenon**

#### *Clance \& Imes, “The Impostor Phenomenon in High Achieving Women”*

[The original 1978 paper. It defines impostor phenomenon as an internal experience of intellectual phoniness despite achievement.](http://paulineroseclance.com/pdf/ip_high_achieving_women.pdf)



#### *Bravata et al., “Prevalence, Predictors, and Treatment of Impostor Syndrome”*

[Major systematic review. Good overview of prevalence, associated traits, and intervention evidence.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7174434/)



#### *Thomas et al., perfectionism, impostor phenomenon, and mental health*

[Good if you relate impostor syndrome to perfectionism, anxiety, depression, or high achievement pressure.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7882132/)



### **Childhood trauma and perfectionism**

#### *Dobos et al. — “What makes university students perfectionists?”*

[Very relevant: childhood trauma, emotion dysregulation, academic anxiety, social support, and perfectionism in university students.](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/sjop.12718)



#### *Verrastro et al. — childhood emotional abuse, neuroticism, perfectionism, and workaholism*

[Relevant if academic pressure later becomes overwork, compulsive productivity, or needing achievement to feel safe.](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-328X/14/4/298)



### **Academic pressure, parental pressure, and “academic trauma”**

#### *Guo et al. — academic pressure, depression, and self-harm from adolescence into adulthood*

[Very relevant to “childhood academic trauma.” This 2026 Lancet Child \& Adolescent Health study examined academic pressure at age 15 and later depressive symptoms/self-harm into early adulthood.](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2352464225003426)



#### *Curran — parental conditional regard and perfectionism*

[Study finding evidence for parental origins of perfectionism, especially when children perceive approval as conditional.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8468227/)
